Mapping Emergency Health Services in Senegal

Defining the scope and service provision capacities of Emergency hospital

ABOUT THE PROJECT

In July 2020, OpenStreetMap Senegal will map all public hospitals in Senegal and update the emergency health services available. It is part of a larger campaign from healthsites.io to define emergency health services across Africa.

We currently have basic information for a limited number of Senegalese health facilities in OpenStreetMap. We will establish building blocks that others can use and advance. We will compare the open data from 197 OpenStreetMap hospitals with that from the open data set developed by the KEMRI-Wellcome research program in 2018 and currently hosted by WHO. With the support of [Afrimapr] ( http://afrimapr.org), we will establish R building blocks to allow personalized analysis in the country using open source packages. We will be monitoring progress in terms of entering additional health facility data into OSM daily. For this, we will use tools such as the Ohsome application developed by HeiGIT and afrihealthsites developed by afrimapr.

WHAT WAS THE STATUS AT THE BEGINNING OF THE PROJECT?

At the start of this project healthsites.io had collected information on 1,221 health facilities in Senegal with most of them based in Dakar.

Most of the health facilities available in the healthsites.io database are pharmacies (48%) with doctors (24%), clinics (14%), hospitals (10%), and dentists (3%) making up the remainder of the entries.

Only partial information is available for all facilities listed in healthsites.io. This means most entries do not list details that are critical to support humanitarian responses.

Project Plan

The project has several collaborators who will each play a role

Emergency health data

Senegal’s OpenStreetMap cartographers will contact hospitals to confirm available emergency services

OpenStreetMap

Health facility data will be uploaded to OpenStreetMap by the cartographers

Open data comparison

The afrimapr team will provide data analysis and comparison with other open health facility data

Open licensing

All materials, including blog posts, images, code, and data created in this project will be released under open licenses

Historical comparison

The HeiGIT team will facilitate historical comparisons with OSM data via their tool - OHSome

Share experience

The team will provide updates and lessons learned via blog posts and a project report
